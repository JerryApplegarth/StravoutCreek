Stravout Creek Mining Adventures

The Stravout Creek Mining Adventures started in 2002 as a family operation. While the mining is just a summer weekend operation, the family has had great times and adventures on the claim.

;

More Mining Stories

This picture shows our first setup with larger equipment. This equipment is called a trommel. The material is added at the back end with a tractor. Water is added and as the trommel turns slowly the material works its way forward and out the end. As the material gets near the end it moves over screens that size it down.

The smaller material then falls into a sluicebox. The sluicebox(near the workers legs) will capture the gold because it is much heaver than the rest of the material being washed through the sluicebox.

The Trommel

That's my father-in-law standing in front of the sluicebox. His job is to remove any large pieces of material that might get stuck in the sluicebox. The main screen on the trommel will screen dow to 3/4 of an inch(about 2 centimeters). The largest nugget we found would not go through the small screens. We found the nugget in the waste pile(large pile of boulders in front of the trommel).

Note of interest: We found this trommel in a canyon on our claim. It was used for mining in the 1980's. We used a big tractor to pull it from the canyon. We did turn it over on its side once before we got it out of the canyon but it suffered no damage and runs great. We used only a 10hp engine to run the trommel.
