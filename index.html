Stravout Creek Mining Adventures

The Stravout Creek Mining Adventures started in 2002 as a family operation. While the mining is just a summer weekend operation, the family has had great times and adventures on the claim.


Signs

Here is one of the many signs found on the claim. This sign has been used for a long time to signify that this area of BLM land is under a current mining claim.

Looking for Nuggets

Here is a small trommel used mostly for sampling a prospect. Once a good prospect is found then larger equipment will be used.

Working the Trommel

Here, we are working with the small trommel, sampling material for amount of gold per yard. It takes about an hour to run 1 yard of material.

Digging for Gold

Two men are digging in the ground looking for gold. Once good color is found, bigger equipment will be used.

Maps

Here is a Platt map, it shows the legal description of a mining property.

Nugget the Dog

Nugget, my dog, was my best friend as I explored the 79 acers of rugged terrian. She was always in the lead making sure it was safe for me to travel.

Prospecting

Prospecting involves a lot of walking, digging, and sampling of material. Here, my friend and I, explor a side creek looking for signs of gold.

Sluicebox

Here a larger sluicebox is being setup for a production run. This box will run about 5 yards per hour of material.

Classifier

Here is a larger classifier used for mining gold. This classifier will run about three yards per hour. A tractor will dump material on the classifier.
